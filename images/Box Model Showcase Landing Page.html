Understanding the Box Model

In CSS, every element is treated as a rectangular box. The Box Model describes how these boxes are rendered on the page, including their content, padding, border, and margin. Understanding this model is crucial for effective layout and spacing in web design. Padding is the space between the content and the border. The border is a line around the padding and content. Margin is the space outside the border, separating the element from other elements.
